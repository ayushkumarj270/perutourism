Discover Peruvian Cuisine

Delve into the rich flavors and culinary traditions that make Peruvian food a global sensation.

Key Ingredients in Peruvian Cuisine

The foundation of Peruvian cuisine lies in a variety of unique and fresh ingredients:

  • Potatoes: Peru is the birthplace of the potato, with over 3,000 varieties grown across the country. Potatoes are used in many dishes, from soups to side dishes.
  • Corn: Corn (or maize) is another staple of Peruvian cuisine, used to make everything from savory dishes to beverages. The different varieties of corn come in many colors, including red, purple, and yellow.
  • Quinoa: This ancient grain, originally cultivated by the Incas, has seen a resurgence worldwide due to its high nutritional value.
  • Aji: Aji, a type of chili pepper, is used to add heat and depth to many dishes. It is a key ingredient in sauces, soups, and marinades.
  • Seafood: With its extensive coastline, Peru is home to some of the freshest seafood in the world, especially in cities like Lima.
  • Herbs: Fresh herbs like cilantro, parsley, and mint are commonly used to season food and add fresh flavor.

Popular Peruvian Dishes

Ceviche

Ceviche is one of Peru’s most iconic dishes. It consists of raw fish marinated in freshly squeezed lime juice, seasoned with chili, cilantro, and onions. It is often served with side dishes like sweet potatoes, corn, or plantain chips. Peruvians usually eat ceviche as a starter or a light lunch.

Lomo Saltado

Lomo Saltado is a traditional Peruvian stir-fry that combines marinated beef, onions, tomatoes, and fried potatoes. This dish reflects the Chinese influence in Peruvian cuisine, as it combines stir-frying with indigenous ingredients. It is usually served with rice.

Aji de Gallina

Aji de Gallina is a creamy chicken stew made with aji amarillo (yellow chili), milk, cheese, and walnuts. It’s a comforting and flavorful dish, typically served with rice and potatoes.

Peruvian Drinks

Pisco Sour

Pisco Sour is Peru's national cocktail, made from Pisco (a type of brandy), lime juice, egg whites, simple syrup, and bitters. It's a refreshing drink that's perfect for special occasions.

Chicha Morada

Chicha Morada is a non-alcoholic drink made from purple corn, flavored with pineapple, cloves, and cinnamon. It's a sweet and refreshing beverage, often enjoyed with meals.

Agua de Amaranto

Agua de Amaranto is a drink made from the amaranth plant. This traditional beverage is popular for its nutritional value and is often served chilled, especially in rural parts of Peru.
